ReCompress

Query-aware rewriting beats deletion — at 8.5× fewer tokens.
A 1.5B model, audited against itself.

Parth Sanjay Kshirsagar · Kartikey Pandey

The problem

Every token costs — and most of them are noise

Single prompt (RAG): 10 passages retrieved, ~2 relevant. Sending all 10 wastes ~80% of the budget on distractors.

Multi-turn: resending full history every turn makes cost grow O(n²) — a 50-turn chat sends 100k+ tokens for ~5k of real info.

context = [passage 1] [passage 2] ...
  8 distractors + 2 that matter

→ the model has to find the needle,
  and you pay for the hay.

The insight

Deletion has a ceiling that rewriting doesn't

bear-1.1 — deletion

Deletes low-value tokens, char-for-char. Fast, verbatim, query-agnostic, cacheable. By design: "nothing is paraphrased or generated."

ReCompress — rewriting

Reads the question, drops irrelevant passages, and rewrites the rest densely. The abstractive, query-aware regime deletion cedes.

Not a competitor — the complement. We took up exactly where bear's design leaves off, and distilled it into a small model.

What we built

One system, two acts

Act 1 — single-shot

Query-aware compressor distilled from a DeepSeek teacher into Qwen2.5-1.5B + LoRA. Runs offline, ~cents/call.

Act 2 — Re:Zero multi-turn

A flat ~300-token memory (protected facts + checkpoint + delta). The Act 1 model compresses the checkpoints.

DeepSeek teacher → 5k pairs → LoRA on a Modal H100 → eval vs bear → wire into Re:Zero. ~$10 total.

Results · across benchmarks

Significant where it's hardest

HotpotQA ✓ 2Wiki ✓ MuSiQue n.s. SQuAD n.s.   — significant on multi-hop-with-distractors; honest about the rest.

We stress-tested our own headline

The win survives an independent judge

Teacher + solver were both DeepSeek — a reviewer's first attack. Re-scored with Claude Sonnet (independent of both): Δ +0.288 vs +0.285 in-family. Not a same-family artifact.

The differentiator · we audited ourselves

Much of the win is span-selection — we measured it

Redact the gold answer span and re-solve: our F1 drops 65% vs bear's 31%. So the edge is largely "keep the answer-bearing span at a 3.5% budget where deletion truncates it" — better selection, not better reasoning. Reported, not hidden.

Act 2 · multi-turn, and a self-correction

Flat context — once we cut our own dead weight

Counting overhead, our LLM checkpoint-trigger ("Echidna") was 98% useless. Replaced with a free rule → 4.2× cheaper than uncached naive by 20 turns. (Honest: never beats a KV-cached agent on raw tokens.)

How it's cheap · the distillation story

Three attempts: wash → overfit → win

v1 under-data (n.s.) → v2 overfit → v3: regularized, early-stopped, significant. We report all three. The 1.5B recovers ~64% of the frontier teacher's margin, offline.

Honest limitations

What we did not prove

  • Significant on 2 of 4 benchmarks — MuSiQue & SQuAD are directional (n=50).
  • Much of the F1 is the answer span itself (mask test) — selection, not reasoning.
  • Multi-turn beats uncached naive only; a KV-cached agent wins on raw tokens.
  • The student is autoregressive — ~1.8× bear's latency (for 9.4× fewer tokens).

Reporting our own negative results is the thing we'd protect most.
